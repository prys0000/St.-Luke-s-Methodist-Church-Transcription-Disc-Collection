# Condition Report

**Collection:** St. Luke's Methodist Church / KTOK transcription discs
**Recording dates on labels:** Monday, May 5, 1947 and Tuesday, May 6, 1947 (multi-disc sets; e.g., Disc #4, Disc #6)
**Origin noted on labels:** Municipal Auditorium (Oklahoma City)
**Carrier:** 16" lacquer instantaneous discs, 33⅓ RPM, "Start Inside," KTOK preprinted labels with typewritten program data

---

## Overall Condition Prior to Treatment

| Condition issue | Severity | Discs affected |
|---|---|---|
| Paper sleeve bonded to lacquer surface | Severe | May 5, 1947 set (documented) |
| Palmitic acid crystalline bloom | Severe | Collection-wide; heaviest on May 6, 1947 Disc 6 |
| Suspected mold through groove field | Moderate–severe | May 6, 1947 Disc 6 (documented) |
| Label staining/transfer (acid migration through sleeve) | Moderate | Multiple |

| Lacquer cracking/delamination | Not observed at treatment | — |

## Disc-Level Notes

### May 5, 1947 disc(s)
- Original kraft sleeve fused to disc surface; sleeve tore on attempted removal, leaving bonded paper over the playing surface. Acid migration visible as staining rings on the sleeve exterior.
- Released via mineral oil method (see `methodology.md`, Stage 1) with no lacquer loss.
- Post-cleaning: uniform gloss recovered; grooves legible; label stable with pre-existing staining.

### May 6, 1947 discs (Disc 4, Disc 6)
- Disc 6 exhibited the heaviest deterioration in the set: dense white crystalline exudate (palmitic acid) intermixed with filamentous deposits consistent with mold, distributed across the full groove field and non-grooved land areas.
- Side-raking light photography documents the depth of buildup within the grooves.
- Vinegar/distilled-water pass produced only partial, superficial reduction (visible as a cleaned band in process photography) — rejected.
- Full Pryse OHS Method cleaned the disc entirely; grooves and land areas returned to legible black lacquer.

## Storage Recommendations Post-Treatment

- Rehouse in unbuffered or pH-neutral archival sleeves designed for grooved discs (no PVC).
- Store vertically, fully supported, no leaning; 16" discs require dedicated shelving.
- Target environment: ~46–50°F for long-term storage or stable cool room conditions, 30–40% RH, minimal fluctuation.
- Prioritize completed digital transfer as the preservation surrogate; lacquer discs are inherently unstable and treatment does not halt plasticizer loss.
